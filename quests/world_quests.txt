WORLD QUEST COMPENDIUM
=======================
170 quests across 17 settlements. Each quest: title, Location (settlement-specific,
named + ID), NPC(s), and a branching Scenario with multiple paths the quest
generator can expand into full option trees.

Factions: 1 = Order, 2 = Guild, 3 = Companions, — = unaligned.

1. ROOKBRIDGE HOLLOW                                                  (unaligned)
================================================================================

1.1 The High Span Ledger
  Location: The Bolt Works (94)
  NPC: Maren Bolt (Blacksmith); secondary: Old Jeck (92)
  Scenario: Maren has pulled three failed anchor pins from secondary crossings
  on the Broken Road (101). The fracture patterns are deliberate. She wants the
  pins walked to Old Jeck for a second opinion, then tagged replacement hardware
  planted on the next targeted crossing so whoever tampers marks themselves.
  Paths:
    (a) Deliver pins to Jeck, plant the tagged hardware, stake out the next
        sabotage and catch the saboteur on a swaying platform.
    (b) Skip Jeck; take the pins directly to the Bridgemaster-equivalent at the
        Drop Market and escalate publicly — faster, but Maren loses control of
        the investigation.
    (c) Sell the information to Doss Halver (quest 1.10); the saboteur goes
        unpunished, Maren loses trust, reward doubled in coin.
  Reward hooks: Maren's favor, Bolt Works forge discount, optional enemy:
  a cornered saboteur on a Rootfall Tier platform.

1.2 Still on the Rope
  Location: The Rope Hall (95)
  NPC: Pike Grenn (Descent Crew Foreman); optional: Vell Sutter (95)
  Scenario: A descent worker went down three days ago on a line still clipped,
  still swaying in the updraft. Pike will pay to recover a body or a reason.
  Paths:
    (a) Solo descent on the original line — shortest, but the Rope-Thing in the
        Lower Mist can tether the harness without notice.
    (b) Rig a parallel second line (requires Rope Hall crew cooperation and a
        bribe or favor to Vell) — safer, but alerts Doss Halver's people who
        watch the Rope Hall traffic.
    (c) Refuse the descent; bring back only the clipped-off end of the line and
        a fabricated story — Pike pays half, his grief closes off further work
        in the Rope Hall for the rest of the act.
  Hazards: Rope-Thing (new enemy, reels prey upward a handspan at a time until
  the line goes slack), delayed echoes, voice-mimicry of the missing worker.

1.3 Roots That Listen
  Location: The Rootfall Tier (91)
  NPC: Tessa Cane (Salvager)
  Scenario: Tessa wants the Root Network behind the cracked Rope Hall wall
  charted — which roots are load-bearing, which are opportunistic. Roots
  respond to sustained vibration.
  Paths:
    (a) Slow and careful — tap-test with a tuning hammer, map over two days,
        full pay, no collapse.
    (b) Fast cut-and-mark — chainsaw-style severance, half the time, triggers a
        localized Rootfall Tier shift that endangers three homes (Maren's shop
        among them).
    (c) Bring Barrek (from Grainfall concept, or a Hollow equivalent) as a
        co-surveyor — shares the pay but produces a map detailed enough to sell
        to Doss or to use in quest 1.10.
  Hazards: root-tightening ambush if three wrong taps in a row; falling debris
  from the Rope Hall wall above.

1.4 The Quiet Below
  Location: The Mist Basin (93)
  NPC: Hask (Mist Basin Watch)
  Scenario: Hask's seven-day log shows the ravine's ambient subsonic hum has
  stopped. He wants a listener to carry a weighted bone cone (Hask's Cone —
  new item) to three depths at three specific hours.
  Paths:
    (a) Complete all three readings honestly — the hum is not missing, it has
        moved laterally and downward; Hask's subsequent quest line unlocks.
    (b) Fake the deepest reading to avoid the longest descent — detectable by
        Hask on examination of the cone's condensation pattern; he pays, but
        will not hire again.
    (c) Let a third party (e.g. Guild surveyor from Sigg's network in Brackwater
        Reach, if narrative allows) ride along and split findings — faster, but
        compromises Hollow secrecy.
  Hazards: the "hour older than the ropes measured" time anomaly at the
  deepest depth — minor stat debuff on return, clears after a full rest.

1.5 Rented Ears
  Location: The Sway Cells (97)
  NPC: Sera Wight (Sway Cells Manager); secondary: Linn Bray (96), Doss Halver
  Scenario: Sera's guest ledger at the Sway Cells names three people who paid
  in Doss Halver's coin and stayed only as long as secondary crossings took to
  fail. She will sell the ledger at the Drop Market at dusk.
  Paths:
    (a) Buy the ledger for Linn Bray — she uses it to move against Doss
        publicly; violence on the walkways follows.
    (b) Buy the ledger for Doss Halver — he destroys it quietly; Hollow
        politics remain stable but three more crossings are sabotaged before
        the act closes.
    (c) Play both buyers — sell, deliver a forgery, keep the real ledger for
        use in 1.8 or 1.10. High reward, high hostility from whichever side
        catches on first.
  Hazards: rooftop chase across swaying walkways; thrown weapons; one
  scripted near-fall.

1.6 The Old Quarry Mouth
  Location: The Old Quarry Mouth (92)
  NPC: Old Jeck (Retired Bridge Builder)
  Scenario: Jeck buried a cache forty years ago — original bridge-builder
  tools, a ledger of deliberate flex points, a sealed bottle he won't describe.
  The tunnels have shifted. He'll trade the location for a promise the ledger
  goes to Maren, not Doss.
  Paths:
    (a) Keep the promise — full cache to Maren; Jeck dies peaceful two acts
        later and leaves a second cache coordinate in his will.
    (b) Sell the ledger to Doss — Maren disowns the player publicly at the
        Drop Market; Doss's coin is generous but the Bolt Works closes to you.
    (c) Keep the ledger for yourself — opens personal crafting recipes for
        anchor hardware; Jeck learns and refuses further contact.
  Hazards: collapsed shafts, nesting cliff-things, a cave-in puzzle that
  requires reading the flex-point ledger from the wrong side.

1.7 The Fee That Wasn't
  Location: The Drop Market (96)
  NPC: Corvin Dale (Drop Market Vendor); antagonist: caravan master on the Rim
  Scenario: Corvin owes an off-Hollow caravan master now waiting on the Ravine
  Rim with an armed escort. The debt must be paid in Maren's reinforced hooks
  (six). Maren will not sell him any at any price.
  Paths:
    (a) Convince Maren — requires favor from 1.1 or 1.3; she sells at cost.
    (b) Forge substitutes using Jeck's old cache tools (requires 1.6 complete)
        — the creditor doesn't test them until two days gone; Corvin stays in
        business; the creditor becomes a recurring antagonist.
    (c) Rob the Bolt Works — fast, generates a crime flag inside the Hollow;
        Maren becomes hostile; Corvin pays top rate.
    (d) Negotiate directly with the creditor on the Ravine Rim — a two-choice
        dialogue (intimidate / offer alternative goods). Success skips the
        theft entirely.
  Hazards: Ravine Rim ambush if negotiation fails; Bolt Works caretaker if
  option (c) is chosen.

1.8 The Wrong Weight
  Location: The Rope Hall (95)
  NPC: Vell Sutter (Rope Hall Crew Chief)
  Scenario: Vell condemned a rope last week. It is back in service. Someone in
  her crew uncut the mark and re-hung it — a bribe signer. The condemned rope
  is rated for the High Span's central sling. A loaded caravan crossing would
  be catastrophic.
  Paths:
    (a) Work a single shift under cover — identify the signer by
        handwriting/knot style; Vell fires and blacklists them.
    (b) Cut the rope yourself overnight — stops the disaster, exposes nothing,
        Vell grateful but suspicious.
    (c) Let a caravan be intercepted on the approach instead — requires Linn
        Bray's cooperation (from 1.5 or 1.9); the political fallout reshapes
        Drop Market power; Vell never knows.
  Hazards: nighttime Rope Hall patrol; one bribed crewmate may draw a knife
  if cornered.

1.9 The Broken Road Caravan
  Location: The Broken Road (101)
  NPC: Linn Bray (Caravan Liaison); caravan drivers (silent)
  Scenario: A caravan on the Broken Road approach has stopped for two days.
  No fires, no signals. Linn wants eyes on it before Doss sends his own and
  rewrites the story. The drivers sit in a circle facing outward, eating
  nothing. The Stillness (new passive condition) transfers by proximity past
  a count of two hundred.
  Paths:
    (a) Observe from distance, return with report — safest, Linn acts
        politically, caravan goods are lost.
    (b) Enter the circle, retrieve the manifest book and one driver's
        personal kit — exposure risk: a stat debuff that lingers until next
        sleep cycle.
    (c) Burn the caravan — Linn refuses payment; Doss pays double and the
        event is never officially recorded.
    (d) Bring Hask (from 1.4) to document the Stillness scientifically —
        requires 1.4 (a) or (c) complete; generates a research reward and
        unlocks deeper Mist research later.
  Hazards: the Stillness itself; Doss's scouts arriving at dusk if the
  player lingers.

1.10 The Span Will Hold
  Location: The High Span (90)
  NPC: Doss Halver (High Span Controller); rival controller on Ravine Rim
  Scenario: A rival controller on the eastern approach is bribing Maren's
  crew into declaring the High Span unsafe, which would shift toll rights.
  Doss wants the rival's intermediary identified and discouraged before the
  next council at the Ravine Rim. Catch: the rival is right. The Span is
  unsafe.
  Paths:
    (a) Full Doss loyalty — identify and beat the intermediary, testify at the
        council, Span stays open; a caravan collapse occurs in act 3.
    (b) Flip on Doss at the council — use evidence from 1.1/1.3/1.8 to prove
        the Span is unsafe; Doss becomes a persistent enemy; the Hollow loses
        a trade week but survives.
    (c) Play both — accept Doss's coin, then leak the council testimony to
        Linn Bray. Highest reward, highest hostility; all three major NPC
        factions (Doss, Linn, Maren) will be pinned on a single reputation
        track for the rest of the act.
  Hazards: intermediary is a competent duelist; the council itself is a
  social encounter with failure penalties.

2. HOLLOWMERE VAULT                                                   (unaligned)
================================================================================

2.1 The Shifting Wing Inventory
  Location: The Unmapped Wing (105)
  NPC: Adri Coss (Settlement Coordinator)
  Scenario: Three residents' workshops in the Unmapped Wing no longer end
  where they used to. Their most important items must be retrieved before the
  wing is sealed — a sealed crate, a ledger, a child's memory-box.
  Paths:
    (a) Chalk-and-string mapping method — slow, reliable, all three retrieved.
    (b) Ask Pell (Deep Vault Guide) for a shortcut route — fast, but Pell
        names the player as his client afterward (triggers 2.4 early).
    (c) Retrieve only two items, claim the third was already gone — the
        resident eventually discovers the lie; Adri withdraws future hires.
  Hazards: rooms re-sequencing mid-retrieval; one sealed corridor that opens
  and closes on a ten-minute cycle.

2.2 Water That Went Elsewhere
  Location: Lower Water Channels (106)
  NPC: Lura Denn (Water Systems Monitor)
  Scenario: A new ankle-deep corridor must be traced to its terminus with
  sound markers every fifty paces. The water is moving toward something.
  Paths:
    (a) Trace to the terminus and return — the destination is a chamber with
        a warm, pulsing floor. Report accurately.
    (b) Seal the corridor from the entry side — faster, stops the water,
        Adri and Gavven both approve, Lura refuses further work.
    (c) Let Gavven's Guild surveyors escort the trace — splits the pay,
        surveys are formalized, unlocks Guild faction favor.
  Hazards: sudden current shifts; a section of ceiling that drops silt on a
  delayed timer.

2.3 The Archive Rearrangement
  Location: Archive Alcoves (107)
  NPC: Gavven Harsk (Guild Expedition Coordinator)
  Scenario: A full memory-sweep of the Archive Alcoves is required before
  Gavven decides whether to seal them. Touch nothing. The sweep reveals a new
  object mid-shelf — warm, pulses once when observed.
  Paths:
    (a) Sweep honestly, leave the object, report — Gavven seals the alcoves.
    (b) Remove the object — triggers proximity effects in the Central Hall
        (103) that last one game-day; Thessa Ring (2.9) will offer to buy it.
    (c) Describe the object falsely — Gavven catches the lie by cross-
        referencing with Drenn's journal (2.7); reputation loss with Guild.
  Hazards: the object itself (no direct damage; subtle stat pulses on
  proximity).

2.4 The Guided Descent
  Location: Threshold Chamber (109)
  NPC: Pell (Deep Vault Guide)
  Scenario: Pell names the player as his client for a single expedition past
  the Threshold into the Deep Corridors, crossing three Sealed Sections (111)
  without hesitation. Conditions: do not speak in the final chamber, do not
  ask what Pell retrieves, do not look directly at him on the return.
  Paths:
    (a) Obey all three conditions — full pay, Pell offers a second expedition
        later; a permanent non-hostile companion gesture in Hollowmere.
    (b) Break one condition — Pell finishes the trip in silence, pays half,
        never hires again.
    (c) Ambush Pell in the final chamber and take whatever he retrieves — the
        item is real and valuable; something in the Deep Corridors follows the
        player for the rest of the act.
  Hazards: whatever is in the final chamber; what follows if condition (c).

2.5 Miri's Shortcut
  Location: Echo Gallery (108)
  NPC: Miri Cole (Independent Salvage Coordinator)
  Scenario: Miri's new route bypasses every Guild-watched corridor, passing
  through the Echo Gallery at an unmonitored hour. Miri's own voice answers
  her from ahead; she ignores it.
  Paths:
    (a) Follow Miri silently, carry the load — standard salvage pay.
    (b) Answer the Echo Gallery voice — Miri aborts the run; later she will
        never take the player on salvage again.
    (c) Report Miri's route to Gavven — Guild payoff; Miri becomes hostile
        and starts intercepting the player's trades.
  Hazards: the Echo Gallery itself (psychological, not lethal); a Guild
  surveyor patrol if the timing is miscalculated.

2.6 The Winch Line
  Location: Sinkhole Entrance (102)
  NPC: Jonn Half (Sinkhole Entrance Operator)
  Scenario: The primary winch cable has wear patches in places rope shouldn't
  wear — as if something rides it up at night. Inspect the shaft walls from
  harness at three elevations between midnight and dawn.
  Paths:
    (a) Three elevations, three observations — confirms the Climbers (new
        enemy: pale, thin, flat against the shaft wall, only move when you
        blink).
    (b) Set bait and trap on the cable — catches one Climber; Jonn rewards
        heavily; the Climbers collectively respond the next night.
    (c) Replace the entire cable secretly overnight — no data, no enemies
        confirmed, Jonn suspects you are hiding something but cannot prove it.
  Hazards: the Climbers; the harness rig itself if rushed.

2.7 The Surveyor's Journal
  Location: Threshold Chamber (109)
  NPC: Drenn Vars (Boundary Surveyor)
  Scenario: Drenn's eighth journal is missing. Last seen in the Unmapped Wing
  four days ago. It records the figures' recent behavior. Thessa Ring wants
  it too.
  Paths:
    (a) Retrieve and return to Drenn — he shares a single line from the
        journal as thanks; unlocks a Guild sub-quest in act 3.
    (b) Sell to Thessa — she uses it in 2.9; Drenn withdraws from his post
        for a week, reducing Threshold safety.
    (c) Copy the journal, return the original — highest reward, slowest
        path, requires one day at the Record House equivalent and a bribe
        to Adri for the materials.
  Hazards: the Unmapped Wing re-sequencing; Thessa's hirelings if option (a).

2.8 Old Kess Will Speak
  Location: Central Hall (103)
  NPC: Old Kess (Retired Vault Researcher)
  Scenario: Kess will describe what she believes the Vault contains — after
  three unremarkable objects (a chipped cup, a length of wire, a smooth stone)
  are retrieved from three specific Mapped Corridors at the exact moments
  their configurations match her memory. Timing is the whole quest.
  Paths:
    (a) Retrieve all three at correct timings — Kess tells the full story;
        unlocks a late-game Deep Corridors encounter.
    (b) Retrieve wrong-timing versions — Kess notices immediately; tells a
        partial, misleading story; the late-game encounter changes character.
    (c) Refuse the quest and buy Kess's account in coin — possible only
        after completing 2.7 (a) and reading the relevant line.
  Hazards: Mapped Corridor configuration shifts; a timing puzzle if option (a).

2.9 The Resealed Chamber
  Location: Sealed Sections (111)
  NPC: Thessa Ring (Enchanter)
  Scenario: A chamber Thessa drew material from for two years sealed itself
  overnight. Her theory: the seal responds to quantity removed. Her fix:
  return three items of equivalent mass — Thessa's Weighed Offering (new
  item: three lead-core stones).
  Paths:
    (a) Place the stones; seal opens; Thessa enters alone and emerges with
        new material (quest 2.3 object, if retrieved, counts as a fourth
        stone and has unintended effects).
    (b) Refuse to participate; Thessa hires a rival; the seal opens violently
        in act 3 and a chamber creature is released.
    (c) Swap one stone for a heavier/lighter object — sabotages Thessa's
        experiment; the seal remains shut; Thessa pays partial and becomes
        hostile.
  Hazards: whatever is on the other side of the seal.

2.10 The Permitted Hour
  Location: Central Hall (103)
  NPC: Osser Vale (General Goods Vendor); escort: Pell (optional)
  Scenario: Osser hasn't been deeper than the Central Hall in nine years. He
  will pay to visit one of the Mapped Corridors, once, at an hour of Pell's
  choosing. He needs to confirm that something he thinks he imagined isn't
  still there.
  Paths:
    (a) Escort alone — cheap for Osser, risky; what he remembers is waiting,
        and escorting him back becomes a combat retreat.
    (b) Bring Pell (requires 2.4 (a)) — the corridor rearranges around Pell's
        presence; Osser sees nothing; he pays full but is unsatisfied.
    (c) Lie to Osser — tell him the corridor is empty without going. He
        believes you; in act 3 he returns alone and does not come back.
  Hazards: what Osser remembers — a minor but unique encounter, nearly
  unbeatable in direct combat; the quest rewards avoidance.

3. GRAINFALL TERRACE                                                  (unaligned)
================================================================================

3.1 The Seventh Wall
  Location: The Lower Terraces (116)
  NPC: Farr Jutte (Seventh Terrace Manager); antagonist: Old Parrek
  Scenario: Farr needs the seventh wall reinforced for one more season without
  a full rebuild. Old Parrek refuses to supply the right stones on principle.
  Paths:
    (a) Convince Parrek — requires 3.10 progress or a social check; legitimate
        materials, full integrity.
    (b) Steal from Parrek's private stock at the Stone Warrens (120) — fast,
        triggers a Parrek hostility arc and a Trainer's Yard confrontation.
    (c) Improvise with Cliff Base (122) rubble (requires Barrek's cooperation
        from 3.4) — unorthodox but effective; Danna Sohr disapproves because
        it changes the terrace's water flow profile.
  Hazards: partial collapse during construction if path (c) is chosen poorly.

3.2 The Upper Channel Climb
  Location: The Spillway (118)
  NPC: Danna Sohr (Lead Water Engineer)
  Scenario: Sediment is building above the settlement faster than Danna's
  crews can clear it. A climber must plant sediment baffles at three anchor
  points on the cliff face above the main channel. One anchor is cracked and
  will fail under a specific weight threshold; Danna does not know which.
  Paths:
    (a) Climb solo — one anchor fails; a reflex check catches the climber or
        the quest ends in injury and partial pay.
    (b) Bring a second climber (Corren Alsh can be hired) — shared pay, both
        climbers at risk on the failing anchor; better overall survival odds.
    (c) Test each anchor with a weighted dummy first (requires 3.10 Parrek
        knowledge or a Trainer's Yard consultation) — safest, slowest, full
        pay.
  Hazards: cliff goats interfering with the ropes; wind shear.

3.3 The Burrows Below
  Location: The Stone Warrens (120)
  NPC: Lenna Cade (Pest Control Specialist)
  Scenario: A fresh burrow entrance in the Stone Warrens has ignored every
  trap. Lenna suspects the pests — the Undertalkers (new enemy: small,
  silent, collectively intelligent diggers) — communicate. She needs a
  watcher with sharp hearing, and no kills until she says so.
  Paths:
    (a) Full patient watch — hear the Undertalker pattern, Lenna adapts her
        traps; the burrow is sealed humanely; they relocate to a new terrace.
    (b) Kill on sight — ends the immediate quest fast; the colony's sibling
        colony emerges under the Trainer's Yard a week later.
    (c) Attempt to parley (requires a specific item or a prior Undertalker
        encounter) — unlocks a unique minor faction for later quests.
  Hazards: the Undertalkers themselves if provoked; a warrens collapse if
  they coordinate a dig at the wrong moment.

3.4 Old Foundations
  Location: The Cliff Base (122)
  NPC: Barrek (Structural Surveyor)
  Scenario: Barrek has found tool marks at the Cliff Base older than the
  settlement's records. He wants a single witness for a descent to a specific
  fissure. If the witness agrees, he shares his findings with the council. If
  not, he burns his maps.
  Paths:
    (a) Witness honestly and agree — the findings go to the council;
        unlocks a settlement-wide political event in act 3.
    (b) Witness and disagree — Barrek burns his maps; the knowledge is lost,
        but Silla Greave (3.5) rewards the player for the outcome.
    (c) Take the maps covertly before Barrek decides — full knowledge, full
        betrayal of Barrek; he becomes hostile after discovery.
  Hazards: loose rock on the descent; one minor hostile creature in the
  fissure.

3.5 The Redirect Vote
  Location: The Upper Terraces (114)
  NPC: Silla Greave (Council Representative)
  Scenario: Two families on the Upper Terraces must be flipped to Silla's
  side on the channel-redirect vote. One message requires physically entering
  a corner of the Stone Warrens Farr Jutte has told Silla's people to stay
  out of.
  Paths:
    (a) Deliver all three messages correctly — the vote passes Silla's way;
        Farr becomes hostile; the terraces gain water, the Warrens change.
    (b) Sabotage one message — vote fails; Silla blames the player; Farr
        quietly rewards.
    (c) Take both bribes (Silla + Farr) — a two-way double-cross that
        requires a specific dialogue sequence; max coin, max hostility.
  Hazards: a Warrens patrol loyal to Farr if option (a).

3.6 The Caravan That Didn't Arrive
  Location: The High Ridge (123)
  NPC: Wendt Hallis (Trade and Logistics Coordinator)
  Scenario: A grain caravan due last week hasn't appeared. Find it. The
  caravan is intact, the drivers unharmed, and they have decided they are
  not coming to Grainfall. They have been persuaded of something.
  Paths:
    (a) Talk the drivers back — requires piecing together what persuaded
        them (a dialogue tree with three key pivots); full caravan returns.
    (b) Take the goods by force or stealth — the caravan returns without
        drivers; Wendt pays, but the drivers' faction spreads a hostile
        rumor across the High Ridge trade route.
    (c) Leave them and accept the loss — honest report; Wendt loses
        standing; a replacement caravan arrives with a surcharge.
  Hazards: the drivers themselves if option (b); whatever persuaded them,
  if the player probes too deeply.

3.7 Metta's New Strain
  Location: The Mid Terraces (115)
  NPC: Metta Dorn (Supplemental Food Producer)
  Scenario: Metta cultivates a fast-growing mushroom from Cliff Base rubble.
  It doesn't behave like fungus when cut. She needs a batch tested on
  someone outside the settlement before offering it as emergency food.
  Paths:
    (a) Eat it yourself — a stat-altering buff/debuff for one game-day;
        Metta pays and documents.
    (b) Find a test subject (a drifter at Clifftop Common (119), a caravan
        worker) — moral weight; consequences if the subject is harmed.
    (c) Destroy the strain; report it as dangerous to Silla or Farr — Metta
        becomes hostile; the supplemental food supply drops for the next
        season.
  Hazards: the strain itself; Metta's discovery if option (c).

3.8 The Trainer's Drill
  Location: The Trainer's Yard (121)
  NPC: Corren Alsh (Combat and Safety Trainer)
  Scenario: Corren is running a fallback drill assuming the settlement is
  attacked at Clifftop Common and must retreat through the Warrens. He needs
  a competent outsider to play attacker — no pulled strikes, real pressure.
  Item: Trainer's Blunted Spear (new item; heavy, leather-wrapped, still
  breaks ribs).
  Paths:
    (a) Play the attacker honestly — rib-breaking combat, reputation gain,
        unlocks Corren as a hireable escort for 3.2 or later quests.
    (b) Pull strikes — Corren notices; pays half; declines future work.
    (c) Defeat Corren outright — unlocks a secret training track and a
        unique technique; Corren loses face publicly.
  Hazards: Corren himself; the drill students if things escalate.

3.9 The Overhang Sound
  Location: The Overhang Caves (124)
  NPC: Hesta Varn (General Goods Vendor)
  Scenario: Hesta's shop is carved into the cliff behind the Mid Terraces.
  For two weeks, a sound at night from the Overhang Caves — not wind, not
  animal, not natural. She will pay for someone to sit inside for one night
  with a lit candle and tell her what it is. She does not want the cave
  sealed.
  Paths:
    (a) Sit the full night — identify the sound source (a resonance
        phenomenon tied to the Undertalkers' colony in 3.3, or a wind
        pattern, or a lone creature — generator should pick one).
    (b) Leave early — Hesta pays for whatever report is produced; the sound
        continues; a later quest hook.
    (c) Seal the cave despite instructions — Hesta withdraws custom;
        whatever was inside finds a new exit in the Stone Warrens.
  Hazards: the sound source itself; candle discipline (a failed check
  extinguishes early and aborts the observation).

3.10 Parrek's Last Lesson
  Location: The Broken Shelf (125)
  NPC: Old Parrek (Retired Terrace Builder); observer: Danna Sohr
  Scenario: Parrek will pass his mortar technique to one student. The test:
  rebuild a section of the Broken Shelf using only his methods, while he
  watches from above with a telescope. If it holds a measured weight at
  sunset, the knowledge transfers.
  Paths:
    (a) Pure Parrek method — slow, correct, the wall holds; Parrek transfers
